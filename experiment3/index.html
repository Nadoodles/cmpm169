Imitate

Prior to starting the assignment, I was looking through different types of generative art. As I was doing research, I came across the two images shown above. From the second picture, I believe that there are several lines starting in the center and then they rotate at different angles, potentially random angles. The left image definitely has central circles that spasm outwards.

Integrate

For starters, I am going to use this code, http://www.generative-gestaltung.de/2/sketches/?01_P/P_2_1_1_04 and expand on it. So far, there’s only one, and the image is set in the center. The lines moves outwards and randomly, which I like, but I will definitely update it over time.

Innovate

  • Step 1: One thing that I added is make the art shrink and expand. The reason why I did this is because in the art that I was inspired by, everything was different. There was no regularity, so starting on this one, I figured I should know how to resize the art. I promise, the images aren’t expanded on docs, they resize in the code.
  • Step 2: In this step, I create multiple of these generative art, that are spaced randomly in the canvas.
  • Step 3: They’re all different sizes now. IN this step, I just made sure that all the circle sizes are different.
  • Step 4: Instead of having a default 5 shapes, it makes one by one every 5 seconds, up to 20 shapes. I definitely want to have this as a starting point, and maybe after 20 seconds, it can disperse and make a new generative art. There’s also random colors for each shape.
  • Step 5: After 5 seconds, 20 new shapes are formed with different colors and locations.
  • Step 6: I changed it so that it’s more frequent, and instead of waiting 5 seconds, if you click on the screen, you can change into new shapes, colors and locations.

Reflection

It took a while because most of the time, they wouldn't revolve around a circle. However, when they finally came out, they looked really nice.
